# M18.3 Baseline Improvement Strategy

==========================================================
## SECTION 1 — BASELINE SUMMARY
==========================================================
The current production GRU regression baseline (NIFTY50/v3.0) successfully executes end-to-end following the resolution of the target `dtype` truncation bug. However, the model exhibits severe underfitting and conservative prediction behaviour. While predictions are no longer collapsing to a single scalar, the prediction variance remains exceptionally small compared to the target variance. The model fails to capture extreme market moves (fat tails) and produces a negative R² score, indicating that it performs worse than predicting the historical mean. 

==========================================================
## SECTION 2 — IDENTIFIED BOTTLENECKS
==========================================================
1. **Unscaled Regression Targets**
   - **Evidence**: Targets (`return_5d`) are raw financial returns concentrated heavily around 0.008 with a standard deviation of 0.036. Optimizing MSE on micro-scale targets yields extremely small initial gradients, often causing neural networks to plateau into predicting the mean.
   - **Severity**: CRITICAL
   - **Confidence**: HIGH

2. **Fat-Tailed Target Distribution (Kurtosis)**
   - **Evidence**: Target kurtosis is 3.21. The presence of extreme outliers (market shocks) heavily penalizes the standard Mean Squared Error (MSE) loss function, forcing the model to adopt overly conservative predictions to avoid massive quadratic penalties.
   - **Severity**: HIGH
   - **Confidence**: HIGH

3. **Feature Redundancy and Noise**
   - **Evidence**: Pearson correlation analysis shows high redundancy among volatility features (e.g., `volatility`, `rolling_volatility_5`) and near-zero correlation for traditional indicators (`macd_line`, `atr`).
   - **Severity**: MEDIUM
   - **Confidence**: HIGH

4. **Registry Contract Mismatch**
   - **Evidence**: `TrainingOrchestrator` exports the feature scaler as `scaler.pkl`, while `RegistryManager` expects `feature_scaler.pkl`, causing a `MissingArtifactError` upon promotion.
   - **Severity**: HIGH (Infrastructure Blocker)
   - **Confidence**: HIGH

==========================================================
## SECTION 3 — IMPROVEMENT CANDIDATES
==========================================================
1. **Hyperparameters**
   - Increase/decrease sequence length to capture different temporal contexts.
   - Tune learning rate schedule and weight decay.
2. **Architecture**
   - Increase GRU hidden size or add layers (e.g., from 64 to 128).
   - Evaluate BiGRU to capture bidirectional context within the sequence.
   - Introduce spatial dropout.
3. **Feature Engineering**
   - Prune uncorrelated / noisy features to prevent overfitting.
   - Apply PCA or non-linear dimensionality reduction.
4. **Target Engineering**
   - Apply `StandardScaler` to the target to force N(0,1) distribution, then inverse-transform during post-processing.
   - Clip extreme target outliers (e.g., at 1st and 99th percentiles).
5. **Loss Function**
   - Switch from MSE to Huber Loss (Smooth L1 Loss) to reduce sensitivity to financial outliers.
   - Switch to Mean Absolute Error (MAE).
6. **Optimization**
   - Adjust gradient clipping thresholds.
7. **Training Strategy**
   - Implement curriculum learning (train on low-volatility periods first).

## SECTION 5 — CONTROLLED EXPERIMENT PLAN
==========================================================
### Experiment 1: Target Scaling
- **Variable changed**: Apply standard scaling (N(0,1)) to `y_batch` during dataset loading, and inverse-transform predictions during metric evaluation.
- **Reason**: Stabilizes gradient flow by normalizing the loss scale, preventing the model from collapsing to the mean.
- **Expected outcome**: Model achieves a positive R² and captures higher variance in predictions.
- **Success metric**: `val_r2` > 0.0.
- **Rollback criteria**: Validation loss divergence or `NaN` gradients.

### Experiment 2: Registry Contract Resolution
- **Variable changed**: Rename artifact key from `scaler.pkl` to `feature_scaler.pkl` in `TrainingOrchestrator._export_artifacts()`.
- **Reason**: Fixes the broken registry promotion pipeline.
- **Expected outcome**: Seamless promotion of the candidate model to the registry.
- **Success metric**: `RegistryManager` successfully registers the model without throwing `MissingArtifactError`.
- **Rollback criteria**: Unforeseen downstream dependency failures in Inference.

### Experiment 3: Huber Loss (Smooth L1)
- **Variable changed**: Replace `nn.MSELoss()` with `nn.HuberLoss()` in the training pipeline.
- **Reason**: Financial returns have high kurtosis. Huber loss acts as MSE for small errors and MAE for large errors, preventing outliers from dominating gradient updates.
- **Expected outcome**: Improved MAE and more accurate predictions for the central mass of the distribution.
- **Success metric**: Lower `val_mae` compared to the scaled baseline.
- **Rollback criteria**: Overall performance degradation or failure to converge.

### Experiment 4: Feature Pruning
- **Variable changed**: Drop the 5 least correlated features (`macd_line`, `return_lag_1`, `atr`, `volume_change`, `dividends`) from the TensorBuilder.
- **Reason**: Reduces input noise, allowing the GRU to focus on highly informative volatility and momentum signals.
- **Expected outcome**: Faster convergence and slightly improved validation loss.
- **Success metric**: Equal or better `val_loss` achieved in fewer epochs.
- **Rollback criteria**: Drop in `val_ic` or `val_r2`.

==========================================================
## SECTION 6 — RECOMMENDED ORDER
==========================================================
1. **Experiment 2 (Registry Contract Resolution)**: Must be executed first to unblock full pipeline automation and metric tracking.
2. **Experiment 1 (Target Scaling)**: The most critical mathematical fix required before any hyperparameter tuning can yield valid results.
3. **Experiment 3 (Huber Loss)**: Follows target scaling to handle the fundamental fat-tailed nature of the targets.
4. **Experiment 4 (Feature Pruning)**: Only attempt feature space reduction once the core objective function is stable and well-conditioned.
