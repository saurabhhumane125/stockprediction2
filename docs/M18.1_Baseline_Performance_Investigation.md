## SECTION 3 — METRIC ANALYSIS
==========================================================
- **RMSE (0.0401)**: Root Mean Squared Error. Measures the square root of the average squared error. Although it looks small in absolute terms, it is large relative to the scale of 5-day returns. Unacceptable.
- **MAE (0.0309)**: Mean Absolute Error. Measures the average magnitude of errors. Unacceptable.
- **R² (-0.1798)**: Proportion of variance in the dependent variable predictable from the independent variable. A negative value proves the model makes predictions that are worse than a horizontal line through the target mean. Unacceptable.
- **Information Coefficient / IC (0.0122)**: Spearman rank correlation between predictions and targets. A value near zero indicates virtually no monotonic relationship between what the model predicts and what actually happens. Unacceptable.
- **Residual statistics**: Errors are roughly centered but have a standard deviation (0.0399) that exceeds the standard deviation of the targets themselves (0.0369).

## SECTION 9 — TARGET ENGINEERING REVIEW
==========================================================
**Target**: `return_5d` (5-day return)

- **Advantages**: It is stationary, scale-independent (works across different stock price ranges), and financially meaningful as a trading horizon.
- **Disadvantages**: It is highly noisy, leptokurtic (fat tails), and its variance dominates its mean.
- **Prediction difficulty**: Because the target mean is extremely close to zero, a neural network optimizing for Mean Squared Error (MSE) will naturally gravitate towards predicting ~0 to minimize loss safely, unless strong, clear signals exist in the features.
